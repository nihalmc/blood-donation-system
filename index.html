About Blood Doners

Blood donors are individuals who voluntarily donate a portion of their blood for medical purposes, such as transfusions or the creation of life-saving medical products. Donors are screened to ensure that their blood is safe for transfusion, and donation is usually done at blood banks or blood donation centers. The process typically involves a health history review, physical examination, and blood tests. It's important for blood donors to be healthy, hydrated, and meet eligibility criteria set by blood banks or health organizations.
